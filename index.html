Junior Penetration Tester
Also known as: Jr. Pentester · Entry-level Pen Tester · Pen Test Analyst · Associate Pentester
A Junior Penetration Tester performs authorized security tests on systems, networks, and applications — finding vulnerabilities and writing reports that help organizations fix them before real attackers strike.
ENTRY LEVEL 0–2 YEARS EXP HIGH DEMAND

// common myths & brutal truth
✗
"I just need to learn Metasploit and I am a hacker"
Metasploit is one tool. Real pentesters understand what happens underneath every exploit. Start with fundamentals, not frameworks.
✗
"CEH is enough to get hired"
CEH is theory-heavy and widely considered weak by hiring managers. eJPT or OSCP carries far more weight.
✗
"Report writing is not important — finding bugs is what matters"
A finding you cannot explain clearly is worthless to a client. Juniors who write clean reports get promoted faster.
✗
"I need a degree in computer science first"
Most practicing pentesters are self-taught. Labs, certs and a strong portfolio matter far more than a degree.
